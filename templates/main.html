What is neonatal jaundice ?

Neonatal jaundice is a common condition that affects newborn babies, characterized by high levels of bilirubin in the blood, which leads to yellowing of the skin and whites of the eyes . Bilirubin is a substance produced when red blood cells break down, and it is normally processed by the liver and eliminated from the body. However, in newborns, the liver may not be fully developed, leading to a buildup of bilirubin. Most cases of neonatal jaundice are harmless and resolve without treatment within a few weeks . However, in severe cases, high levels of bilirubin can cause brain damage or other complications, so it is important to monitor and manage the condition appropriately. Treatment options may include phototherapy (exposure to special lights that help break down bilirubin), exchange transfusion (replacing the baby's blood with donor blood), or other measures as needed. If you suspect that your baby has jaundice, it is important to talk to your healthcare provider, who can perform a physical exam and evaluate the baby's bilirubin levels.
